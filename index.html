About the Project

Understanding our community's housing needs and developing actionable solutions

Housing Challenges

Lake City and Hinsdale County face significant challenges in providing affordable and workforce housing. As Colorado's most remote county with 96% federally owned land, our community requires tailored housing solutions.

Project Goals

This comprehensive housing plan will identify housing demand, develop financial strategies, assess project feasibility, and create clear implementation steps to move from planning to action.

Community Involvement

Your input is essential to this process. Through surveys, public meetings, and ongoing engagement, we're working to ensure that housing solutions reflect the real needs of our community.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the housing plan process

What is the Comprehensive Affordable Housing Plan?

The Lake City Comprehensive Affordable Housing Plan is a strategic initiative to address housing challenges in our community. The plan will identify housing needs, develop financial strategies, assess project feasibility, and create clear implementation steps to increase affordable housing options for residents and workers.

Why is this plan necessary?

Lake City and Hinsdale County face unique housing challenges due to limited developable land, seasonal population fluctuations, and workforce housing shortages. This plan will provide actionable strategies to address these challenges and ensure long-term housing affordability and availability for our community.

How can I get involved?

There are several ways to participate:
- Complete the community housing survey
- Attend public meetings and workshops
- Join the Housing Working Group (contact Town Hall for details)
- Share your housing experiences and ideas with the project team
Your input is essential to developing effective housing solutions!

What is the Lake Fork Project?

The Lake Fork Project is a planned 28-unit housing development on Henson Street that will be a key focus of this housing plan. The project is supported by a DOLA grant under the More Housing Now (MHN) program, and our team will be working to develop feasible financing and implementation strategies to bring this project to fruition.

When will the plan be completed?

The Comprehensive Affordable Housing Plan is scheduled to be completed by October 2025. However, our approach focuses on implementation throughout the process, meaning that actionable steps and strategies will be developed and potentially implemented before the final plan is delivered.
